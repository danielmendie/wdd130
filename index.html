Akwa Ibom, Nigeria

Akwa Ibom State, located in the coastal southern region of Nigeria, is one of the country’s oil-rich states and a major contributor to Nigeria’s economy. Created in 1987, its capital is Uyo, a fast-growing city known for modern infrastructure and hospitality. The state is home to diverse ethnic groups, primarily the Ibibio, Annang, and Oron, with a rich cultural heritage expressed through traditional festivals, music, and cuisine.Bordered by the Atlantic Ocean, Akwa Ibom is blessed with natural resources, beautiful beaches, and tourist attractions such as the Ibom Plaza, Ibeno Beach, and the Godswill Akpabio International Stadium. It is also widely recognized for its high literacy rate, peaceful environment, and vibrant spirit of enterprise.
